MTF: My Top Favorites

My Top 3 Book Picks

I'm not particularly a fan of movies and I'm more of a bookish person, so I'll just share my top favorite books:

The third spot goes to Noli Me Tangere by Dr. Jose P. Rizal. This Filipino literary canon depicts the life of the Filipinos during their colonization under the Spanish regime. This book became my favorite during my third year in high school because it was really well-written and insightful. It awakens the "makabayan" spirit in anyone who reads it seriously.

Next is the Percy Jackson Series by Rick Riordan. I love this book because I'm a fan of Greek myths and this book gives a modern, wholesome, and funny twist to the sometimes morbid and immoral stories of the Greek gods and goddesses.

Finally, the top spot goes to the Harry Potter Series by J.K. Rowling. Who wouldn't know the story of the orphaned boy who lived under the cupboard and is constantly maltreated by his aunt's family until he discovered that he was a wizard and is well-known in the Wizarding world as The-Boy-Who-Lived. Rowling's take on the story reels you into the magical world and makes you want to believe in magic. Rowling's style is the best so far.

The Top 3 Songs of my Playlist

The third spot goes to Dati by Sam Concepcion. This song is relatable to the 90's kids like me. Although I haven't experienced the lovey-dovey story depicted in the song, almost every part of the song stirs up childhood memories. It is really relaxing and nostalgic.

Next is Runaway by the Corrs. I like the sound of it. It is relaxing and well, sweet. It makes me want to sleep.

Finally, my top spot goes to Canon and Gigue in D major. It was originally composed by Johann Pachelbel around the 18th century as a chamber music and has now evolved into a classical piece with many versions. I know most people see classical music as a sort of lullaby, but for me it's not. As like the other two, this piece is relaxing and it is my favorite because it helps me think and focus. Although I say it is relaxing, the tempo and beat of the music sends a surge of excitement within me, thus preventing me from falling asleep and staying out of focus. A good listening material for hell week and cramming nights.
